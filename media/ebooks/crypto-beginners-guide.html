Chapter 02

The Only 5 Terms You Need to Know

📖 Before You Read

You don't need to understand the engineering. You need to understand the concepts well enough to make good decisions. These 5 terms cover 90% of what you'll encounter.

Term 1 — Blockchain

Think of it as a glass staircase where every step is visible to everyone and impossible to erase. Each step records who sent what to whom, chained together permanently. Thousands of computers worldwide maintain identical copies — no single company controls the history.

Example: When Anna sends €50 in Bitcoin to Ben, that transaction becomes a new glass step that computers across the globe verify instantly.

Term 2 — Wallet

A digital keyring, not a purse. It doesn't store coins — it stores access codes that prove ownership on the blockchain. Your wallet shows your balance but the actual coins live on the shared ledger.

Example: Maria's phone app shows she owns 0.5 ETH, but the ETH lives on the blockchain — her wallet holds the cryptographic key proving ownership.

⚠ Term 3 — Private Key (Most Important)

Your master password. A unique code that unlocks your funds. Lose it and your crypto vanishes forever — no customer service line can recover it.

Tom wrote his 12-word seed phrase on paper, stored it in a fireproof safe. When his phone died, he recovered €10,000 instantly. His friend Mike ignored backups and lost everything when his laptop crashed.

Term 4 — DeFi

A 24/7 vending machine for financial services. No bank tellers, no opening hours, no permission needed. Smart contracts execute loans, trades, and interest payments automatically.

At 3 AM on a Sunday, Luca deposited ETH as collateral and borrowed €3,000 in stablecoins — without filling out a single form or speaking to a human.

Term 5 — Market Cap

Price × total supply. Measures the total value of a cryptocurrency. A higher market cap means more money already invested — it reveals relative size, not investment quality.

Dogecoin costs €0.08/coin but has a large supply. Bitcoin costs €60,000/coin but has a limited supply. Compare market caps, not sticker prices.

Chapter 05

The 3 Biggest Mistakes Beginners Make

Mistake 1 — Investing More Than You Can Afford to Lose

Real case: A Lisbon restaurant manager liquidated his €40,000 life savings into Luna coins in 2022. The protocol collapsed within 72 hours. He lost everything.

The fix — The 5% Rule: Calculate 5% of your liquid net worth. If you have €20,000 in savings, your crypto maximum is €1,000. Set this cap before opening any exchange. If you cannot afford to see that number hit zero tomorrow, reduce your position.

Mistake 2 — Panic Selling During Dips

Real case: When Bitcoin fell from €58,000 to €28,000 in May 2021, retail investors flooded exchanges to sell. They locked in 50% losses. Six months later, Bitcoin hit €67,000.

The fix: Automate discipline. Set monthly recurring buys. Delete trading apps during crashes. Remove price alerts from your phone. Volatility is the entry fee for gains. Selling during red candles turns temporary dips into permanent losses.

Mistake 3 — Following Signal Groups

Real case: The "Wolf of Crypto" Telegram group charged €200/month for "guaranteed 10x calls." Subscribers bought when the admins pumped; admins dumped seconds later. Most signal groups are pump-and-dump schemes.

The fix: If someone knew tomorrow's price, they would buy islands — not sell €50 tips. Verify everything independently. Read whitepapers. Check on-chain data. Follow protocol developers, not influencers with affiliate links.

Chapter 06

Keeping Your Crypto Safe

Security is not a product you buy — it's a set of habits you build. Spend 30 minutes on these steps now. They prevent losing thousands later.

🔐
Google Authenticator 2FA (Required)
Never use SMS 2FA — SIM-swapping attacks specifically target SMS codes. Download Google Authenticator, scan the QR code on MEXC. Save backup codes offline.
📋
Withdrawal Whitelist (Required)
Restricts withdrawals to pre-approved addresses only. If a hacker breaches your login, they cannot drain funds to their own wallet.
🔩
Hardware Wallet for €1,000+ (Recommended)
Ledger or Trezor — store private keys offline. Buy only from the manufacturer's official website. Never buy second-hand (may be preloaded with malware). Cost: €60–€150.
🚫
Phishing Defense (Always)
Bookmark your exchange URL. Type it manually. Never click links in emails claiming "account suspension." No legitimate company ever asks for your seed phrase — treat every such request as fraud.
🆘 If You Get Hacked: Damage Control

1. Disconnect the compromised wallet from all apps immediately via Revoke.cash
2. Transfer remaining assets to a NEW wallet with a fresh seed phrase
3. Document everything — screenshots, timestamps
4. Accept that stolen crypto is likely gone. Blockchain transactions are irreversible. Focus on securing what remains.

Quick Reference

20 Terms, One Sentence Each

Blockchain
A shared digital ledger where every transaction is recorded permanently and visible to all.
Wallet
Software or hardware that stores your private keys and shows your crypto balance.
Private Key
A secret code proving ownership of your crypto — lose it, lose access forever.
Seed Phrase
12–24 words that regenerate your wallet — store offline, never digitally.
Exchange
A platform to buy/sell crypto — MEXC, Binance, and Kraken are examples.
SEPA
European bank transfer system — free, arrives in 1–2 business days, best EU deposit method.
Maker / Taker
Maker = limit order (sets a price, 0% fee on MEXC). Taker = market order (buys now, 0.05%).
DCA
Dollar Cost Averaging — buying a fixed amount on a schedule regardless of price.
Market Cap
Price × total supply — measures relative size of a crypto, not quality.
Altcoin
Any cryptocurrency other than Bitcoin — ETH, SOL, XRP are all altcoins.
Hardware Wallet
Physical device (Ledger, Trezor) that stores keys offline — use for amounts over €1,000.
2FA
Two-factor authentication — adds a second code required to log in (use Google Authenticator).
KYC
Know Your Customer — EU-required identity verification to use crypto exchanges.
Gas Fee
Transaction cost on Ethereum — paid in ETH, varies by network congestion.
DeFi
Decentralized Finance — financial services run by smart contracts, not banks.
Stablecoin
Crypto pegged to fiat (USDT = $1) — used to hold value without volatility.
Halving
Bitcoin event every ~4 years cutting new supply in half — historically precedes bull markets.
Smart Contract
Self-executing code on a blockchain — runs automatically when conditions are met.
Bear / Bull Market
Bear = prices falling -20%+ sustained. Bull = prices rising. Crypto cycles: 2–4 years.
Whitepaper
A project's technical document explaining what it does — always read before investing.
