Techniques & Tutorials
🪂 Paragliding
🌀
Thermalling Basics
Intermediate
1
Find thermal triggers: south-facing slopes, dark fields, road junctions in low wind
2
Enter at 45° angle — don't enter downwind. Bank to 30° immediately on entering lift
3
Tighten turn where lift is strongest (variometer peaks). Loosen in sink
4
Watch other gliders and birds — they're your best thermal markers
⚡ Pro tip: Weight shift first, brake second. Smoother turn = less energy loss
🤸 Skydiving
💨
Track Position
Advanced
1
Arch aggressively: shoulders back, hips pushed forward, head up
2
Arms 45° back along body, not delta — delta kills horizontal speed
3
Legs straight together, toes pointed — creates one aerodynamic surface
4
Target 50m horizontal separation/1,000ft altitude before deploying
⚡ Pro tip: Practice tracking on solo jumps first — not on group exits
🪁 Kitesurfing
🌊
Water Relaunch
Beginner Essential
1
Never fight the kite — let it drift to the edge of the window naturally
2
Steer one wingtip skyward using the rear line of that side — patience
3
Once wingtip is up, gently steer kite toward 12 o'clock in one smooth motion
4
Rider: stay on your back in body drag position until kite is stable overhead
⚡ Pro tip: Pull both rear lines equally when depowering — prevents one-side collapse
🪂 Paragliding
⚡
SIV — Simulated Incidents
Expert — Over Water
1
Front collapse: push hands forward, glider surges — pump brakes once firmly on recovery
2
Asymmetric collapse: outside brake to 3/4, weight-shift away, steer toward open sky
3
Spiral: release outside brake, shift weight inward, wait for glider to build speed naturally
4
Reserve throw: find handle, throw hard and high, clear canopy from body
⚡ Only practice SIV over water with instructor present. No exceptions.
🤸 Skydiving
🎯
Canopy Accuracy Landing
Intermediate
1
At 1,000ft: confirm wind direction from surface indicator (windsock, smoke)
2
Set up perpendicular base leg at 600ft — not too close, not too wide
3
Final turn at 300ft: smooth, no more than 90° — low turns kill people
4
Flare timing: begin at ~20ft, full stall at ~5ft — match glide path to ground
⚡ Never sacrifice a good spot for style. Land off target if needed — always.
🪁 Kitesurfing
🦅
Big Air Jumping
Advanced
1
Load the lines: edge hard on approach, kite at 45°, build speed over 3–4 pumps
2
Pop: extend back leg explosively, redirect kite to 12 with front hand simultaneously
3
Apex: pull bar to chest, tuck knees, look at landing zone — not at kite
4
Landing: sheet out slightly, spot landing 2sec early, absorb with bent knees
⚡ Height is free. Distance costs energy. Focus on vertical, not horizontal.
⚠️ Essential Safety Rules — Never Negotiate These
🌬️
Know your limits in windParagliding: don't launch above 25–30kph for EN-B wings. Kiting: size down aggressively — better to be underpowered.
🔧
Inspect gear every sessionLines, risers, brake toggles, reserve pins. Skydiving: AAD active, reserve in date, 3-ring clean. No gear = no fly.
🏥
Always fly with insuranceRescue helicopter costs €15,000+. DAeC, FFVL, or local club insurance is €80/year. It's not optional.
📱
File a flight plan + emergency contactAlways tell someone your launch site, intended route, and expected return. Take an emergency beacon on XC flights.
🌤️
Weather is not negotiableNever fly with thunderstorm cells within 50km. Check Windy, Meteo Blue, and local reports. Conditions can deteriorate in 20 minutes in the Alps.
📚
Continuous training is non-negotiableSIV course every 2 years for paragliders. Canopy course every year for skydivers. Kitesurfing instructor refresh after winter gap.
