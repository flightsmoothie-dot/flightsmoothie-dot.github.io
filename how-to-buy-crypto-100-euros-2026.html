The €100/Month DCA Strategy

Dollar Cost Averaging (DCA) means buying a fixed amount on a regular schedule regardless of price. It's the single most effective strategy for most people who aren't professional traders.

Why it works:

  • You buy at all price levels — some high, some low — averaging out to something reasonable
  • You remove the emotion from the decision. No more "should I buy now or wait?"
  • Over 4+ year cycles, DCA into BTC has been profitable at virtually every entry point in history

After You Buy: What To Expect

The price will move — up or down — within hours of your purchase. This is normal. Crypto is volatile by nature. What matters is what you do when it moves.

If it goes up 10% on day 1: Good. Don't sell. You bought because you believe in the long-term case. A 10% move in week 1 is noise.

If it drops 15% in week 1: Also normal. Don't sell. If anything, this is the DCA opportunity — week 2 is a chance to buy at a better price. The traders who made the most money in crypto are the ones who bought more when the price was scary, not less.

Check it weekly, not daily. Daily price watching increases anxiety without providing useful information for long-term investors.

FAQ

Can I buy less than €100?

Yes. MEXC minimum order is approximately €5-10 depending on the pair. There's no rule requiring you to start with €100 — it's just a round number that covers meaningful learning without significant risk.

Do I need to pay tax on crypto gains?

Tax rules vary by EU country. Most EU countries tax crypto gains as capital gains above certain thresholds. Germany has a notable exception: crypto held for 1+ year is tax-free. Check your specific country's rules or consult a tax advisor. Keep records of your purchases and sales from day one.

What if MEXC gets hacked?

MEXC has an insurance fund similar to Binance's SAFU. No exchange is 100% immune to hacks. For amounts under €5,000, the convenience of keeping funds on an exchange is reasonable. For amounts above €10,000, use a hardware wallet for the majority of your holdings.

Should I buy Bitcoin or Ethereum first?

Both at once, using the 70/30 split above. Don't overthink the timing — you can't perfectly time the market, and trying to costs more (in stress and missed opportunities) than it saves.
