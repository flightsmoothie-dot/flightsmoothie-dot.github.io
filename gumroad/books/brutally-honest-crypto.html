Bitcoin: The Only One You Need to Understand First

Before you look at any other cryptocurrency, you need to genuinely understand Bitcoin. Not the price. The protocol.

What Bitcoin Actually Is

Bitcoin is a decentralized ledger maintained by approximately 15,000 nodes worldwide. No single entity controls it. No government can shut it down without shutting down the internet. Transactions are irreversible. Supply is capped at 21 million coins by code — not by policy.

That last point matters more than anything else in crypto. Unlike every fiat currency in history, Bitcoin cannot be inflated. The supply schedule is known decades in advance.

21M
Maximum Bitcoin supply — ever. Currently 19.7M mined.

The Halving Cycle

Every ~4 years, the reward for mining new Bitcoin is cut in half. This is called the halving. Every halving has historically been followed by a bull market within 12–18 months. The last halving was April 2024. We're currently in a post-halving cycle.

This doesn't guarantee price increases. But it does reduce new supply at a time when demand from institutional investors continues to grow.

How to Buy Bitcoin Safely

  • Proof of reserves (they actually hold the assets they claim)
  • Regulated in major jurisdictions
  • Strong security track record (no major hacks)

Chapter 3

Altcoins: The Opportunity and the Graveyard

Bitcoin is the store of value. Ethereum is the programmable money layer. Everything else is a bet — some informed, most not.

Ethereum: The Other Solid Foundation

Ethereum is the infrastructure layer for decentralized finance, NFTs, and smart contracts. It's not perfect (gas fees can be high during congestion), but it has the strongest developer ecosystem by far.

Owning ETH is effectively owning a stake in the infrastructure of the decentralized internet. That's the bull case. The bear case is that a technically superior competitor could displace it — so far this hasn't happened despite years of "Ethereum killers."

How to Evaluate Any Altcoin

The 5-Question Framework
1. What problem does it solve? If you can't answer this in one sentence, that's a red flag.
2. Who is solving it? Anonymous teams with no track record are higher risk. Doxxed founders who've built and shipped products are lower risk.
3. Who uses it today? Real users, not just speculators? Check actual on-chain activity, not just price and volume.
4. What's the token unlock schedule? Many projects dump massively when team/VC tokens unlock. Check tokenomics thoroughly.
5. What's the worst case scenario? Can you emotionally and financially handle going to zero? If no, don't invest.

The Red Flags Checklist

  • 🚩 Guaranteed returns promised anywhere in the marketing
  • 🚩 Team is anonymous with no verifiable track record
  • 🚩 Influencers are paid to promote (check disclosures)
  • 🚩 No working product, only a whitepaper
  • 🚩 Token supply heavily concentrated with founders
  • 🚩 "Endorsed by" claims with no verification
  • 🚩 Telegram/Discord full of bots and pump language
  • 🚩 Contract has mint function or blacklist function

Position Sizing: The Rule That Keeps You Alive

The biggest mistake in crypto is over-allocation. The rule:

  • Bitcoin/ETH: max 50–70% of your crypto portfolio
  • Large-cap altcoins (top 20): max 20–30%
  • Small/mid cap: max 10–20%
  • Degens/new launches: max 5% total, max 1% per position

This keeps a catastrophic loss in any one position from destroying your portfolio.

Chapter 4

DeFi: What Works, What Doesn't, What to Avoid

Decentralized Finance promised to replace banks. Some of it has succeeded. A lot of it has blown up spectacularly.

What Actually Works

Decentralized Exchanges (DEXs): Uniswap, Curve, and dYdX genuinely work. You can swap tokens without a centralized party. Lower trust required.

Lending protocols: Aave and Compound have operated without major incident for years. Borrow against your crypto, or earn interest on deposits. Actual utility.

Liquid staking: Lido lets you stake ETH without running a node. Real product, real utility, generates real yield from Ethereum's proof-of-stake.

What's Mostly Hype

Yield farming with double-digit APYs: If a protocol is offering 200% APY, ask where that yield comes from. Usually it's from selling the protocol's own token — which means someone is taking a loss.

Algorithmic stablecoins: After the Terra/LUNA collapse erased €40 billion in value in 72 hours, this category should be treated with extreme caution.

💀
The Brutal Truth About DeFi Yield: Sustainable yields in DeFi range from 3–8%. Anything above 15% involves either extreme risk, selling tokens, or a Ponzi structure. Usually all three.

Smart Contract Risk: The Hidden Danger

Every DeFi protocol is a set of smart contracts. Smart contracts have bugs. Bugs get exploited. In 2023–2024, over $2 billion was stolen through DeFi exploits.

Before using any DeFi protocol, check:

  • Has it been audited by reputable firms (Certik, Trail of Bits, OpenZeppelin)?
  • How long has it been running without incident?
  • Does it have a bug bounty program?
  • Is there insurance available (Nexus Mutual, InsurAce)?

Tax, Security & The Practical Stuff Nobody Tells You

The exciting stuff gets all the attention. The boring stuff determines whether you actually keep your profits.

Crypto Tax: The Reality

In most countries, every crypto transaction is a taxable event. This includes:

  • Selling crypto for fiat
  • Trading one crypto for another
  • Using crypto to buy goods/services
  • Receiving crypto as income or staking rewards

This means if you made 50 trades in a year, you have 50 taxable events to report. Most people discover this when they get a letter from the tax authority.

📊
Solution: Use a crypto tax tool from day one. Koinly, CoinTracker, and Accointing connect to your exchange accounts and generate automatic tax reports. €50/year is much cheaper than a €5,000 tax penalty.

Security: The Non-Negotiables

✅ Hardware wallet for any amount over €1,000
✅ Unique email address for crypto accounts (not your main email)
✅ 2FA via authenticator app (NOT SMS — SIM swaps are common)
✅ Seed phrase written on paper, stored in physical location (NOT digital)
✅ Different passwords for every exchange (use a password manager)
✅ Never click crypto links from Twitter/Discord/Telegram DMs
✅ Verify smart contract addresses on official sites, not search results
